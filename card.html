Northwestern Wolf

Northwestern wolves are the largest wolves in the world and will travel up to 70 miles in one day.

  • Scientific Name: Canis Lupus Occidentalis
  • Average Length: 32-40 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 6-8 years; some live up to 17 years
  • Habitat: North American mountains and forests

Also known as the Mackenzie Valley Wolf, these wolves can grow to be 85-153 pounds. Their grey and white fur keeps them warm in their mountainous habitat. Only the alpha pair reproduce about 4-6 pups. They are carnivores that mainly huntlarge game, such as moose, bison, elk and caribou.
